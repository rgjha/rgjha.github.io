Nature is Physics, rest is stamp collecting - Ernest Rutherford

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at Perimeter Institute for Theoretical Physics in Waterloo, Canada. I completed my Ph.D. from Syracuse University in 2019. More details can be found in my CV. My research is related to the study of supersymmetric gauge theories as non-perturbative formulations of superstring/M-theory via the gauge/gravity duality. I am interested in numerical studies of the maximally supersymmetric gauge theory using Monte Carlo simulations to predict results from the supergravity (SUGRA) side. This is a possible check of the AdS/CFT conjecture. This conjecture relates $\mathcal{N}=4$ in four dimensions and Type IIB string theory on $AdS_{5} \times S_{5}$. However, even when the field theory is non-conformal (in space-time dimensions not equal to four), there is still some region in the coupling space where the conjecture holds. I am also interested in exploring thermal phase structures of multi-matrix models (such as BMN). In recent years, I have started working on tensor networks methods to study lower-dimensional gauge theories such as SU(2) gauge-Higgs models and spin models exhibiting continuous or discrete symmetries such as 2d O(2)/XY, Ising model in a magnetic field, and other interesting models with sign problems which are not tractable by Monte Carlo methods.
